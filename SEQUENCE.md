# CARTA — the sequence: acts, gates, and the way to market

*The sequencing pass. The suite before this one designs the end-state; this
document refuses to let the end-state be built out of order. Six acts, each
with a gate — a measurable, refusable condition that must be true before the
next act begins. The gates exist because the two classic ecosystem deaths are
both sequencing deaths: monetising before being loved, and courting the
second side of a market before the first side works alone. Where a number
appears below it is a target to be earned against real constituents, not a
fact — stated at the precision actually known, per the voice.*

## The doctrine — three rules the whole sequence obeys

- **Every act must be worth shipping even if the next act never happens.**
  The record was whole without the atlas; the atlas must be whole without
  the desks; the desks whole without the doors. No act is a loss-leader for
  a later one.
- **Read-only value precedes write-side asks.** Every constituency first
  encounters CARTA as something already true about them — their page,
  already compiled, already almost right. The ask is never "populate our
  platform"; it is "hold your own pen."
- **Commerce last, and only over proven intent.** The door opens where
  readers already leave pages to buy (`COMMERCE.md`). Tolls follow
  traffic; they never summon it.

## Act I — the record *(built)*

The keeper's instrument: one cup, two contexts, offline, exportable, loved
by its keeper. Done, and permanently load-bearing: the app is the trust
floor every later constituency stands on, and it is never allowed to
degrade into a client of the platform. Its north star stays return rate
(`NORTH_STAR.md`).

## Act II — the atlas *(in progress)*

`VISION.md`'s seven steps: the normalised spine, the identity ladder,
curator ingestion, the two surfaces, the standing, the brew corpus,
discovery over saturated data. One keeper-curator seeds one city and the
lots of one season behind it.

**The gate out of Act II:** the atlas proves its two claims on real data —

- **Chart No. 1 is legible:** one city's specialty scene substantially
  charted (its venues, its roasters, the season's lots behind them) by
  curation alone, and strangers use it read-only without being taught.
- **The page nothing else can render exists:** at least a handful of
  genuine same-green-many-roasters cases live, resolved through the
  ladder, each showing its evidence — the thesis demonstrated, not argued.

## Act III — the signed page

The first trade-side act, deliberately tiny: roasters and venues in Chart
No. 1 claim their pages (`PLATFORM.md`'s ceremony). No desk yet — signing
buys the first-party rung, a monochrome mark, and the right to correct
your own facts. The pitch is a walk-in with the page already open:
*"this is what the record says about you; it is nearly right; here is the
pen."*

**Why the roaster is the wedge:** the same-green page is marketing a
roaster cannot make alone (`CONSTITUENTS.md`), sourcing pride is the
industry's strongest vanity, and one signed roaster makes every venue
pouring them and every importer supplying them adjacent to the graph.

**The gate:** a majority of the chart's serious roasters signed; signed
pages *stay* current unprompted (offerings confirmed within days of
release, not quarters) — the signal that holding the pen has become their
bookkeeping, not our campaign.

## Act IV — the desks

The Roast Desk, Venue Desk, Import Desk in working form: the free floor
first (publishing is always free — `COMMERCE.md`), the paid readings when
they're worth a tool's price. The desks ship when the desk path is
**lighter than the constituent's current workflow** — offering-page
ingestion beats retyping into their own site's CMS; the menu switch beats
the chalkboard photo on three other platforms.

**The gate:** for a meaningful cohort, the desk is the *source* of their
offering or menu data, not a copy of it — measured by which side updates
first when reality changes. Paid conversion healthy on desks whose free
floor is already habit. The Archive's moderation queue stays inside its
curators' capacity at chart three, or expansion pauses (the honest-read
trade in `PLATFORM.md`, honoured).

## Act V — the doors, the programme, the Circular

Commerce, in the order of least corruption risk: the instruments
programme and Gear registry (no ranking surface to corrupt), the Folio
(the keeper's own record, printed), the Circular (floored aggregates),
and last the door itself — buy-through referral where reader intent is
already observed leaving pages toward sellers' counters.

**The gate:** the refusals hold under real revenue — an audit any curator
can run shows ranking blind to door revenue (`PLATFORM.md`'s separated
ledgers), zero sold placement, zero below-floor data egress. If the gate
fails, the door closes until it passes; the sentence to protect outranks
the revenue line, and this document is the receipt.

## Act VI — origin

Last not because it matters least but because it must not be asked to
carry the cold start: the Origin Desk goes live paired — one importer
already on the Import Desk, one station they buy from every year, one
harvest run through the desk end to end, cherry to export split. Free
forever, phone-first, offline-first, ingesting the station's existing
spreadsheets rather than replacing them.

**The gate (and the ecosystem's finish line):** a lot born at the mill —
entered at harvest by the hands that processed it — travels the whole
graph untouched by archaeology: importer split, roaster confirm, venue
pour, keeper reading, all binding to the node origin created. The
direction of writing has inverted. After this act there are no more acts;
there is tending the graph and adding charts.

## The way to market — charts, not campaigns

CARTA expands the way its namesake suggests: **one chart at a time.** A
chart is a city whose scene is dense enough to close the loop locally —
enough roasters, venues, and keepers that each constituency's value
arrives from its own neighbours.

- **Choosing charts:** density of specialty venues per capita, a
  self-regarding roaster scene, and a resident curator who already loves
  the record. The founder's city is Chart No. 1 by definition. The
  natural early set is the trade's proud towns — the Portlands,
  Melbournes, Copenhagens, Seouls — but the criterion is scene density
  and curator quality, never market size.
- **The chart method, each time:** curate first (the atlas useful
  read-only), sign the roasters second, venues follow their roasters,
  desks when updating is lighter than their status quo, doors when
  intent shows. The same sequence as the acts, replayed per city — later
  charts simply run it faster on proven machinery.
- **Origin regions chart differently:** by harvest season and importer
  pairing, not by café density — Act VI's method, repeated per region.

## The competitive read — stated plainly

- **Cropster** owns roastery production telemetry. Complementary by
  design: the desk imports its conclusions and refuses its territory
  (`CONSTITUENTS.md`). Partner posture.
- **Algrano and the sourcing platforms** own green transactions and
  relationships. CARTA keeps the record both sides settle against and
  never transacts — adjacent, and a natural door partner at the green
  layer if ever needed. Partner posture.
- **Consumer brewing apps** (Beanconqueror and kin) hold recipes in
  silos; none holds a shared identity spine or a trade-side constituency.
  Coexist; the keeper's app competes on being loved, not on lock-in —
  full export is the standing dare.
- **Review platforms** (the Yelps and Googles) have venues without a
  coffee ontology — they can say a café is popular, never what is in the
  cup or where it came from.
- **The moat, named:** the identity spine (resolution, not keys — years
  of accumulated adjudications that cannot be scraped), the curated
  correctness of a bounded catalog, scoped first-party attestation, and
  the refusals themselves — a trust position a better-funded entrant
  cannot buy and can only match by matching the restraint, which is the
  one thing growth capital is worst at.

## The measures — one per altitude

- **The ecosystem:** legible spend (`ECOSYSTEM.md`) — the share of coffee
  bought with its road visible at the moment of payment.
- **The graph:** resolution quality — the share of catalog lots at each
  grain, hard-ID and first-party coverage, adjudication backlog.
- **The constituents:** pens held — signed pages current without
  prompting, per chart.
- **The keeper, unchanged and sovereign:** return rate. If the ecosystem
  ever grows while return rate falls, the ecosystem is feeding on the
  instrument, and the sequence stops until that reverses.

Time-in-app, feeds scrolled, and notification opens remain non-measures,
at every altitude, forever.

## The risks, without flinching

- **The two-sided cold start** — answered structurally: the atlas is
  valuable read-only before any trade constituent exists (Act II gates
  on it), so the second side arrives to a set table or the sequence
  simply holds at Act II, which is itself a good product.
- **Moderation swamps curation** — answered by the gate at Act IV:
  expansion pauses rather than the catalog rotting. Slow and right beats
  fast and wrong; it says so in writing now.
- **The door corrupts quietly** — answered by architecture
  (`PLATFORM.md`), audited at Act V's gate, and disowned in advance by
  `COMMERCE.md`'s poison lines. The failure mode is named so it can be
  seen coming.
- **Origin writes prove too hard** — the honest fallback: the graph
  remains archaeology-plus-first-party from the importer down, which is
  already the best record the trade has ever had. Act VI is the summit,
  not the foundation.
- **A platform giant clones the surface** — they can clone pages; they
  cannot clone adjudicated identity, a curated bounded catalog, or a
  decade of refusals. The moat is the discipline; keep it.

## The sentence to protect, at the end of the road

Every act, gate, chart, and refusal above serves one test, and it is the
same test the record was born with: does this help a person — or a whole
trade — know the cup it loves, and find it again?

**We do not sell coffee. We keep the record every sale is made against.**

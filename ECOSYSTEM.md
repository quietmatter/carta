# CARTA — the ledger of the trade

*The third turn. `NORTH_STAR.md` is the record of where CARTA started — one
cup, two contexts, your taste the through-line. `VISION.md` is the record of
where it turned — the lot at the centre, the atlas around it. This is the
record of where it opens: the same spine, extended until every hand a coffee
passes through keeps its own page on it. The commission behind this pass asked
for "the most influential nexus of specialty coffee — full grasp from how the
cherry is grown to how the cup is consumed, driving spend at every level."
This document takes that ambition seriously by taking it quietly. It is the
head of a suite: `CONSTITUENTS.md` designs a product for every actor,
`COMMERCE.md` states how spend moves and how CARTA earns, `PLATFORM.md` grows
the machinery, `SEQUENCE.md` orders the road and names the gates.*

## The three turns

CARTA began as a personal memory. The through-line was *you* — one cup, two
contexts, your taste connecting them.

It turned into an atlas. The through-line became *the coffee* — one green lot,
traced from the ground it grew in to the cup in your hand, your taste a wash
laid over the trace.

It turns a third time here, and this is the last turn, because there is
nothing larger to turn toward: the through-line becomes **the road itself**.
Not one keeper's memory, not one lot's trace — the whole chain of hands a
coffee passes through, each hand keeping its own link of the record, all of it
on one spine. The producer's harvest, the station's processing, the importer's
offer, the roaster's roast, the café's menu, the keeper's cup: one graph,
written at the source of each fact, read from anywhere.

CARTA stops being an app with a database behind it. The graph becomes the
product; every surface — the keeper's instrument, the atlas, the desks the
trade works at — is a reading of it.

## The problem the third turn solves

Today, provenance is **archaeology**. Every fact about a coffee's road is
reconstructed at the last node of the chain, from whatever a bag chose to
print. The atlas proved that compiled identity works — the resolver, the
fingerprint, the propose-and-confirm ladder (`LOT_IDENTITY.md`) can rebuild
the road from downstream copy. But rebuilding is the tell. The information
existed, perfectly, at the moment each thing happened: the station knew the
day-lots it separated; the importer knew which roasters took which boxes of
one lot; the roaster knew its roast date without squinting at a stamp. The
trade destroys its own record downstream and CARTA digs it back up.

The ecosystem inverts the direction of writing. **A fact enters the record at
the node where it is born.** The harvest at the farm. The split at the mill.
The sale at the importer. The roast at the roastery. The pour at the bar. The
reading in your hand. To the one who stands at each node, writing the fact is
bookkeeping — a line in a ledger they already keep somewhere worse. To
everyone downstream, that same line is the archaeology they no longer have to
do. An ecosystem is nothing more mystical than the arrangement where everyone
does their own bookkeeping and everyone else stops digging.

The atlas does not become obsolete; it becomes the **floor**. Compiled
identity is how the record holds where the trade has not yet picked up the
pen — which at the start is everywhere, and even at maturity is a long tail.
The resolver and the signed page are the same spine at two confidences, and
the confidence ladder (`PLATFORM.md`) is built to carry both without lying
about which is which.

## What "the most influential nexus" means, soberly

Not the biggest app. The **reference**. In every trade, influence pools with
whoever keeps the record the others settle against — the chart the pilots
sail by, the registry the ships are entered in, Lloyd's before it was an
insurer, the stud book, the appellation registry. None of those sold the
goods. All of them shaped every sale.

The commission's shorthand was "the Apple of the coffee world," and the
useful reading of that is not scale or sheen. It is three habits:

- **Own the integration points, not the market.** Apple does not make the
  apps or the songs; it keeps the rails they meet the customer on. CARTA does
  not grow, roast, or sell coffee; it keeps the graph on which growing,
  roasting, and selling become legible to each other.
- **Restraint as strategy, not decoration.** The refusals — no ads, no sold
  placement, no inventory — are not brand garnish. They are why every
  constituent can trust the record, and trust is the only moat a record has.
- **Few things, finished.** One graph. One identity spine. A small set of
  surfaces, each complete. The catalog correct before it is large.

Influence, on those terms, is measurable: the share of specialty coffee whose
road is legible end-to-end at the moment someone pays for it. Call it
**legible spend**. It is the ecosystem's north-star measure, and it is the
honest generalisation of the record's old one — *return rate* asked whether a
cup was worth finding again; legible spend asks whether the trade can see
what it is buying, at every link, at the moment of buying. Every constituent
product in this suite exists to raise it, and `COMMERCE.md` exists to take a
quiet toll on it.

## The spine, unchanged

Nothing in the third turn dislodges the atlas's model — it is the reason the
turn is possible. The lot stays the hero, identity stays a compiled
resolution and never a key, the standing stays sourced-or-unread, quality and
traceability stay independent, the overlay stays yours. What the ecosystem
adds is not new truth but new **witnesses**: the people who were actually
standing at each node when the fact occurred, signing their own lines.

In the machinery's terms (`SCHEMA.md`, `PLATFORM.md`): the envelope, the
sighting, the sparse merge, and the compile survive intact. The ecosystem
adds a scoped **first-party** confidence — the station's line about its own
processing outranks a bag's copy about the station, and outranks *nothing*
outside the station's own link — plus the signed page it hangs from, and the
desks the signing parties work at. The Register pattern, worn upstream one
hand at a time, all the way to the cherry.

## The chain, drawn once

```
Producer ─ Processor ─ Aggregator/Importer ─ Roaster ─┬─ Venue ── Reader
 (grows)    (washes,      (pools, moves,     (roasts, │  (pours)   (drinks)
             dries)         offers)          blends)  └─ Shelf ─── Keeper
                                                         (sells)   (brews)
                                                Gear ──────────────┘
                                          (grinds, brews — the instruments)
```

Seven constituencies stand on that line, and each gets a designed product in
`CONSTITUENTS.md`: the producer and processor (one desk — the Origin Desk),
the importer (the Import Desk), the roaster (the Roast Desk), the café (the
Venue Desk), the equipment maker (the Gear Registry and the instruments
programme), the home barista (the keeper's instrument — today's app), and the
reader (the atlas, no account, no logging, ever).

The design law for all seven: **each constituent writes the facts born at its
own node, and reads the whole road.** What you contribute is one link. What
you get back is the chain — and the chain is worth more to every hand on it
than any hand's link is to itself. That asymmetry, not marketing, is why
each constituent picks up the pen.

## The flywheel, stated drily

More readings make the overlay denser. A denser overlay makes the atlas worth
reading. An atlas worth reading is worth a roaster signing its page — its
coffees are already there, compiled from bags, and the only question is
whether the roaster's own line or a bag's copy speaks for it. A signed page
writes first-party facts; first-party facts make the road legible further
upstream; a legible road is what a reader pays a premium against — at the
bar, at the shelf, at the checkout behind a door. Spend that walks through a
door is visible demand; visible demand is the one thing that convinces the
next constituent upstream — the importer, then the station — that the pen is
worth holding. And a station that registers its own harvest births lots at
the top of the chain that every hand downstream inherits instead of
reconstructing.

No step of that loop requires advertising, gamification, or a feed. Each
step is one constituent acting in its own quiet interest against the record.
The flywheel is just bookkeeping compounding.

## The refusals — what CARTA never does

The refusals are the product. Written once here, enforced everywhere, and
`COMMERCE.md` prices around them rather than through them:

- **CARTA never sells coffee.** No inventory, no house brand, no setting of
  prices. The moment the keeper of the record owns a horse in the race,
  every line in the record becomes marketing.
- **Standing is never for sale.** No sold placement, no sponsored discovery,
  no promoted pages. Caliber, rarity, traceability — compiled from
  sourced facts, never picked, never purchased.
- **No ads.** Not native, not tasteful, not "just at the bottom."
- **Readings are never sold.** The overlay is the person's own. Only the
  trade's aggregate view of itself is ever offered, and never below the
  pooling floor (`PLATFORM.md`).
- **No feed, no streaks, no engagement mechanics.** The ritual stays primary;
  CARTA serves the coffee, never the reverse.
- **Never print a number that lied to get there.** Across every surface, for
  every constituent — a roaster's desk obeys the same honesty the keeper's
  brew screen does.

## The sentence to protect

The old seal survives one more turn, with one clause grown:

**We do not sell coffee. We keep the record every sale is made against.**

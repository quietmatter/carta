# CARTA — how spend moves, and what the keeper of the record may charge

*The commerce pass. `ECOSYSTEM.md` set the posture — restraint as strategy,
the refusals as product — and the commission asked that CARTA drive spend at
every level of the chain. This document holds both at once: where spend
actually moves in specialty coffee, how the graph raises it, and the short
list of things CARTA may charge for without corrupting the record it keeps.
Prices below are stated as postures and provisional ranges, not commitments —
the voice forbids inventing precision, and pricing earns precision only from
contact with real constituents (`SEQUENCE.md` gates it accordingly).*

## The theory of spend — legibility, not persuasion

CARTA does not create demand. It removes the illegibility that suppresses
demand that already exists.

Specialty coffee's commercial problem is not that people won't pay more — it
is that at the moment of payment, the reason to pay more is invisible or
unverifiable. A $28 bag and a $14 bag sit on the same shelf making the same
unfalsifiable claims in the same fonts. A café pouring a competition lot
prices it like the batch brew, because the story doesn't survive the
counter. An importer's meticulous traceability dies at the roaster's
re-bag. Every link of the chain under-captures because the link before it
is opaque.

The graph's commercial function is to make the premium **legible at the
moment of spend** — the scan in the aisle, the lot's page open at the bar,
the standing with its evidence one tap deep. Legible spend
(`ECOSYSTEM.md`) is the north-star measure precisely because it is the
commercial one: every point it rises, some purchase happened with the road
visible, and purchases made with the road visible settle higher on it.
That is how CARTA "drives spend at all levels" — not a marketing engine
but a legibility engine, and the trade pays for legibility because
legibility is margin.

## The map of the money

Where money moves in the chain, and CARTA's relation to each flow:

| Flow | Size & shape | CARTA's posture |
|---|---|---|
| Reader/keeper → roaster (bags, D2C & shelf) | the retail heart | **the door** — referral, disclosed |
| Reader → café (cups) | daily, small, vast | drive footfall; never toll it |
| Café → roaster (wholesale) | the trade's backbone | surface leads; never broker |
| Roaster → importer (green) | contract & spot | make offers legible; never transact |
| Importer → origin (green, FOB) | the chain's root | keep out entirely — free desk only |
| Keeper → gear maker (equipment) | episodic, high-ticket | registry + certification; no store |

Two flows are tolled (the door, the programme), two are served by paid
desks beside them, two are deliberately untouched. The rule that produced
this table: **CARTA monetises the legibility around a transaction, never
the transaction itself** — with the one exception of the door, where the
toll is on the *introduction*, is flat-rated, disclosed, and identical for
every seller.

## What CARTA charges for — the whole list

Six lines. Anything not on this list is not revenue; if a seventh line is
ever proposed, it must survive this document's refusals in writing.

### 1 · The door — buy-through referral

Any page a coffee lives on — a roast, a bag put away, a lot — may carry
its **door**: the link out to the seller's own counter. A sale that walks
through carries a referral fee back to CARTA.

- **Same door for everyone.** One rate, published, identical for a
  two-person roastery and a national brand. The door cannot be bought
  wider, moved higher, or coloured brighter.
- **The door never feeds ranking.** Discovery, standing, and the atlas are
  compiled blind to door revenue — enforced by architecture
  (`PLATFORM.md`): the referral ledger is a separate system the compile
  functions cannot read.
- **Posture on rate:** single-digit percent, at or below the affiliate
  norms the trade already tolerates; flat per-introduction pricing for
  wholesale leads if ever tolled at all (and the default is: not tolled —
  wholesale legibility sells the desks instead).
- The checkout is always the seller's. CARTA holds no payment, no
  inventory, no customer-of-record. The keeper's reorder tap lands on the
  roaster's till.

### 2 · The desks — subscriptions for the trade

The Roast Desk, Venue Desk, and Import Desk (`CONSTITUENTS.md`) are paid
tools, priced like instruments, not like ad platforms:

- **Free floor, always:** the signed page, publishing offerings and menus,
  the pin, the door itself. Everything the *graph* needs from a
  constituent is free — a paywall on writing facts would starve the record
  to feed the till.
- **The subscription buys the reading, not the writing:** the corpus view
  on your own coffees, the demand and re-encounter reads, door analytics,
  multi-location tooling, offer-sheet bulk pipelines.
- **One law amended, out loud.** `VISION.md` wrote that the brew corpus "is
  never for commercial use." The ecosystem narrows that law rather than
  silently breaking it: what a desk reads is the same pooled, floored
  distribution the atlas shows any keeper — the fee buys that view
  *assembled over your own offerings*, never privileged access, never an
  exclusive cut, and never a licence to print corpus figures in marketing.
  The corpus serves keepers first; the trade may look over their shoulder,
  standing.
- **Posture on price:** one flat tier per desk, monthly, cancel-any-time,
  in the range of the other tools the constituent already keeps —
  tens-of-dollars monthly for a venue or small roaster, low hundreds for
  an importer or roastery group. No seats arithmetic, no enterprise
  theatre, no sales calls. The price list fits on an index card.

### 3 · The Origin Desk — free, forever, structurally

Not a promotional rate; a design constant, stated once in
`CONSTITUENTS.md` and priced here as zero in perpetuity. The graph's
legitimacy requires the pen to be free where the chain is poorest and the
facts are born. This line appears in the revenue list precisely so that it
can never quietly move to another one.

### 4 · The instruments programme — Gear certification and API

- **Instrument of record** certification: a published honesty standard and
  a conformance process; flat annual fee per maker (not per unit — no
  per-unit royalty, because a toll on every scale sold becomes a quiet tax
  on keepers).
- The write-API licence for hardware and software that writes brews into
  keepers' ledgers with consent.
- The registry itself, and presence in it, stays free — canonical Gear
  serves the corpus's honesty first.

### 5 · The Circular — the trade's aggregate view of itself

A quarterly read, sold to the trade by subscription, named for the market
letters the coffee trade has printed since the 1800s: demand by origin,
process, and roast level; dial-in norms by method cluster; standing and
price-at-door distributions; season-over-season movement. Rules:

- **Aggregate only, floored always** — nothing below the pooling floor
  (`PLATFORM.md`), no keeper-level data at any price, no exclusive cuts.
- Compiled from what the graph can honestly say, with the same
  sourced-or-unread discipline as the standing; a Circular line that
  cannot show its evidence doesn't print. The amended corpus law (§2)
  applies in full: nothing a subscriber reads is finer than what the
  atlas shows anyone.
- Posture on price: hundreds annually — priced for a roastery's planning
  shelf, not a hedge fund's.

### 6 · The Folio — the keeper's one paid object

A bound, printed year of a keeper's own record: their cups, their best
known brews, the roads their coffees travelled, the atlas plates for the
places they kept. Printed on paper worth the record, once a year, priced
like a good book. The keeper's app itself is free forever; the Folio is
the only thing a keeper is ever offered for money, and it is their own
record given back with a spine. (It is also quietly the trust artifact:
a company that will print your data and hand it to you is visibly not a
company that resells it.)

## What is never revenue — the poison lines

Restated from `ECOSYSTEM.md` in commercial terms, because this is the
document that will be under pressure when growth slows:

- **No sold placement, ever.** Not "featured," not "sponsored," not
  "partner" rows. The first purchased pixel of discovery is the last day
  the record is trusted.
- **No ads.** None — not third-party, not house, not "a quiet line at the
  bottom." The refusal in `ECOSYSTEM.md` carries no carve-outs.
- **No selling coffee.** No inventory, no marketplace checkout, no house
  label. Also: no equity or revenue share in any constituent — the keeper
  of the record holds no horse in any race it records.
- **No data sales below the floor.** No keeper-level, venue-level, or
  named-roaster cuts of the corpus to anyone, at any price, including
  makers in the programme and subscribers of the Circular.
- **No paid API tiers that gate honesty.** Public read API stays free at
  civil rate limits; money buys throughput, never truth.

## The shape of the whole, honestly

The consumer side runs near-free by design — the app free, the atlas free,
the Folio a rounding line that pays for its own paper. The engine is the
trade side: desks recurring, the programme steady, the Circular seasonal,
and the door scaling with exactly the thing the whole product exists to
raise — legible spend. Which is the deepest alignment in the design:
**CARTA's only growing revenue line grows only when the road is visible at
the moment someone pays on it.** No engagement to farm, no attention to
sell; the business compounds with the same number the mission does.

Sequencing discipline rides in `SEQUENCE.md`, but its commercial rule
belongs here: **nothing monetises before it is loved, and the door opens
last.** The desks charge only when their reads are worth a tool's price;
the door opens only where readers already leave pages to buy. A toll booth
on an empty road is a signal of desperation; a quiet one on a busy road is
just bookkeeping.

*We do not sell coffee. We keep the record every sale is made against —
and we charge for the lamp, never the road.*

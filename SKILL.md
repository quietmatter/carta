# The goal

CARTA is to become the most influential nexus of specialty coffee — the
reference record of the trade, from the cherry on the branch to the reading in
the hand. Not the biggest app; the record everyone else settles against.

**The through-line has turned twice and lands here:** first it was you (the
record), then the coffee (the atlas), now the road itself (the ecosystem) —
every hand a coffee passes through keeping its own page, on one shared spine.

## The commission, in one breath

Full grasp of coffee as it is produced to the end-consumer's consumption and
everything in between: producer, processor, aggregator, roaster, café, gear,
keeper, reader. One data graph at the core — the lot-keyed spine — informing
every surface built on it. CARTA drives spend at every level not by selling
coffee but by making every purchase along the chain legible.

## The laws that survive every turn

- **We do not sell coffee. We keep the record every sale is made against.**
  No inventory, no prices set, no house brand — ever.
- **Standing is never for sale.** Placement, reach, caliber — compiled from
  sourced facts, never bought, always showing their evidence.
- **The record is the user's.** Offline-first, full export, no lock-in.
  Readings are never sold; only the trade's aggregate view of itself.
- **Never print a number that lied to get there.** No invented precision, no
  unsourced scores, no grind numbers across grinders.
- **Authority is scoped to your own link of the chain.** The roaster is the
  authority on the roast, never on the farm. The road is compiled, not owned.
- **Surface calm, depth on demand.** Quiet luxury; restraint is the strategy.

## The test for any decision

Ask, in order:

1. Does it make some link of the road more legible, or a fact enter the
   record closer to where it was born?
2. Does it keep every refusal above intact — especially under commercial
   pressure?
3. Would it still sound right read aloud in the voice of the keeper of
   records (`VOICE.md`)?

Three yeses, proceed. Any no, redesign or refuse.
